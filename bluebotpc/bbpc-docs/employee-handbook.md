# BlueBotPC Employee Handbook

## Welcome to BlueBotPC

Welcome to BlueBotPC. We are pleased to have you join our team of IT professionals. This handbook outlines our company policies, procedures, and expectations. While comprehensive, this document cannot address every situation. Please consult with management if you have specific questions.

## Company Overview

BlueBotPC specializes in Network Engineering and Managed IT Services, providing comprehensive technology solutions to our clients. Our success depends on maintaining the highest standards of professional service and technical expertise.

## Employment Policies

### Equal Employment Opportunity

BlueBotPC provides equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, marital status, or veteran status.

### Employment Classification

Employees are classified as either full-time (working 32+ hours per week) or part-time (working fewer than 28 hours per week). Additionally, employees are classified as either exempt or non-exempt according to federal and state wage and hour laws.

### Work Hours and Scheduling

Standard business hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Some positions may require different schedules or on-call availability. Specific scheduling requirements will be discussed with your supervisor.

## Compensation and Benefits

### Compensation

Employees are paid bi-weekly. Direct deposit is available and encouraged. Pay rates are reviewed annually based on performance and company growth.

### Benefits

Full-time employees are eligible for:

- Health insurance (effective first of the month following 60 days of employment)
- Paid time off (PTO)
- Professional development opportunities
- 401(k) retirement plan (after one year of service)

### Paid Time Off (PTO)

Full-time employees accrue PTO based on length of service:

- 0-2 years: 12 days per year
- 3-5 years: 18 days per year
- 6+ years: 24 days per year

PTO requests must be submitted at least one week in advance when possible.

## Professional Employee Conduct

### Dress Code

Employees should maintain a professional appearance appropriate for an IT Services Professional. Business casual attire is standard. When meeting clients, business professional attire is required.

### Client Interaction

All client interactions must be conducted professionally and courteously. Maintain client confidentiality and protect sensitive information at all times.

### Technology Use

Company equipment and systems are for business use. Limited personal use is permitted if it doesn't interfere with work duties. Never install unauthorized software or disable security measures.

## Remote Work Policies

### Eligibility

Remote work privileges are granted based on job role, performance, and operational requirements. Not all positions are eligible for remote work due to the nature of their responsibilities.

### Remote Work Requirements

Employees working remotely must:

- Maintain a clean, dedicated, professional workspace.
- Ensure reliable high-speed internet connection.
- Be available during core business hours.
- Attend virtual meetings as required.
- Maintain same level of productivity and responsiveness as in-office.
- Use company-approved VPN and security tools.

### Communication Expectations

Remote employees must:

- Remain accessible during work hours via approved communication channels.
- Respond to messages within established timeframes.
- Keep calendar updated with availability.
- Attend team meetings and client calls as scheduled.
- Document work progress in designated project management tools.

## Security and Confidentiality

### Data Protection

Employees must follow all security protocols when handling client and company data. This includes:

- Using strong passwords.
- Enabling two-factor authentication.
- Encrypting sensitive data.
- Securing physical documents.
- Never sharing credentials.

### Confidentiality Agreement

All employees must sign and adhere to our confidentiality agreement, protecting both company and client information during and after employment.

## Performance Expectations

### Technical Proficiency

Employees are expected to:

- Maintain current knowledge of relevant technologies.
- Complete assigned training and certifications.
- Document work thoroughly.
- Follow established procedures and best practices.

### Customer Service

Provide excellent customer service by:

- Responding promptly to requests
- Communicating clearly and professionally
- Following up to ensure client satisfaction
- Escalating issues when appropriate

## Workplace Safety

### General Safety

Report any unsafe conditions immediately. Follow all safety protocols when working with equipment or at client sites.

### Emergency Procedures

Familiarize yourself with emergency exits, first aid locations, and emergency contact procedures. In case of emergency, ensure personal safety first, then assist others if possible.

## Leaving Employment

### Resignation

Professional courtesy requires two weeks' notice of resignation. Submit a written notice to your supervisor.

### Return of Company Property

All company property must be returned upon separation, including:

- Technology Devices
  - Phone and/or Tablet
  - Laptop and/or Desktop
- Hardware Tooling
  - Low-Voltage Tools
- Documentation
- Access Cards and Keys
- Company-issued Credit Cards

## Conflict of Interest Policy

### Definition

A conflict of interest occurs when an employee's personal interests interfere with their ability to act in the best interest of BlueBotPC or our clients. This includes financial interests, personal relationships, or outside business activities.

### Disclosure Requirements

Employees must disclose:

- Any financial interest in client organizations
- Personal relationships with clients or competitors
- Outside employment or consulting work
- Board positions or advisory roles
- Any situation that could create appearance of conflict

### Prohibited Activities

Employees may not:

- Use company resources for personal gain
- Accept gifts valued over $100 from clients or vendors
- Perform unauthorized work for competitors
- Share confidential information with outside parties
- Use position to influence business decisions for personal benefit

### Management of Conflicts

When conflicts arise:

1. Immediately disclose to supervisor
2. Document nature of conflict
3. Develop management plan with supervisor
4. Recuse from related decisions if necessary
5. Update disclosure if circumstances change

### Annual Review

Employees must review and update conflict of interest disclosures annually or when circumstances change.

## Policy Updates

This handbook may be updated periodically. Employees will be notified of significant changes. The most current version is always available from Human Resources.
